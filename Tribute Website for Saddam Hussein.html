Saddam Hussein

1937-2006

Fifth President of Iraq

Saddam Hussein was the fifth President of Iraq, ruling from 1979 until 2003. He belonged to the Ba'ath Party, which emphasized Arab unity and socialism. Under his rule, Iraq saw significant modernization, but also harsh repression and long warsâ€”especially the Iran-Iraq War and the Gulf War. He was captured by U.S. forces in 2003 and executed in 2006 after a controversial trial.

Biography

  • Many Muslims, especially in Pakistan, saw Saddam Hussein as a brave Muslim leader who opposed Western domination.
  • His execution on Eid-ul-Adha was viewed by many as a symbolic martyrdom, increasing sympathy for him in the Islamic world.
  • Some Pakistanis saw him as a victim of Western injustice due to his stance on Palestine, anti-Zionism, and independence from Western influence.
  • While critics highlight his authoritarian rule, others defend him as a protector of Arab nationalism and Islamic pride.
